XI. Community Tuberculosis Control

Last Updated October 2025

For TB control efforts, it is important that there be a smooth transition from the in-patient to the out-patient setting and close cooperation and coordination of activities among the wide variety of organizations involved in TB patient care, education, and TB control.

  • Patients should be medically stable and ready for discharge.
  • Patients have been initiated on treatment with an appropriate anti-TB regimen (e.g., 4-drug regimen).
  • A referral has been made directly to a local health department liaison for continuation of care and DOT.
  • Patients have their discharge endorsed in the chart prior to discharge by the hospital social worker and the local health department liaison.
